JLPT - Japanese Language - Proficiency Test
(日本語能力試験)-(Nihongo Nōryoku Shiken)
What is JLPT?
1. The Japanese-Language Proficiency Test (Nihongo Nōryoku Shiken) , commonly known as the JLPT, is a standardized assessment designed to measure and certify the Japanese language skills of non-native speakers. It assesses knowledge of the language, reading comprehension, and listening skills.
2. The JLPT is administered by the Japan Foundation for exams held outside of Japan, in collaboration with local partner institutions, and by Japan Educational Exchanges and Services for tests conducted within Japan.
3. The JLPT comprises five separate levels of certification- from N5 to N1, with Level N5 representing the most fundamental and Level N1 the most advanced.
